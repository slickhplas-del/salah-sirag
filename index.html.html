Methodology

How I Build a
Marketing Department

A repeatable framework developed across three department builds. The goal is always the same: a function that produces results without needing me in every decision.

WEEK 1
Diagnose
Meet the CEO and business development lead. Extract the real vision, not the stated one. Map the competitive landscape. Identify what's broken and what's missing. Audit existing assets — pages, content, any prior campaigns.
WEEKS 2–4
Build the Foundation
Define brand purpose, mission, core values, brand promise, target audience, tone of voice, key messages, visual identity — color palette, fonts, graphic elements. Everything the team will use as their north star before a single ad runs.
MONTH 2
Build the Team & Systems
Hire for the gaps — not for roles, for outcomes. Implement ClickUp workflows: task assignment, review cycles, reporting, attendance accountability. Brief every team member with quarterly content planning so direction is never lost.
MONTH 3+
Execute & Scale
Launch performance campaigns with A/B testing. Weekly reports up to leadership. Man-to-man sessions with each team member. Cross-train team members across functions. The department runs when I'm not in the room.

Case Study 01

Onyx
Real Estate

Real Estate · Egypt
The Situation

Joined as a content creator. Found a company with active social pages, no brand identity, no target audience definition, no performance marketing, no lead generation system, no website, and no SEO — and a CEO who wanted results immediately.

The Decision

Proposed and led a full rebrand before running a single ad. Argued to leadership that unbranded lead generation produces unqualified traffic that doesn't convert — and wastes the budget they were eager to spend. They agreed. Within 2 months, the brand foundation was in place.

The Strategy

Tracked which projects the major real estate investment companies were advertising heavily — using their capital as a market signal. If Arabtec or a comparable firm was flooding ads on a project, that project had institutional confidence behind it. Convinced the CEO to concentrate media buying on those same projects. Studied the creative and copy structures of top-performing ads. Built campaign variants with A/B testing across audiences, creatives, and placements. Managed the full media buying process internally — eliminating a lead provider who was charging 10% commission. Replaced their cost with 5% internal spend, saving significant margin on every deal.

Case Study 02

Telephonic BPO —
The 10-Day Crisis

BPO · Employer Branding
The Situation

TELEPHONIC was a BPO operating US and UK sales accounts. A major US solar sales account was at risk of being lost due to insufficient qualified agents. In an all-hands leadership meeting, the CEO stated plainly: if we don't fill the headcount in 10 days, the company closes. 45 people's jobs were on the line.

My Role — Unasked For

This was not my job. My role was marketing manager. The HR lead was new and out of their depth. I raised my hand in the meeting and asked: give me the requirements, I'll run the recruitment ads. I knew how to bring people. I'd done it with clients. This was no different.

The Execution

Built and launched a targeted recruitment campaign using performance marketing principles applied to talent acquisition. Wrote the ad copy. Defined the audience. Set the budget. Monitored daily. In a 10-day window including two days off, the campaign generated over 200 qualified applicants. We needed 40. The account was saved. The company did not close. The remaining applicants were archived into a backup talent pipeline that HR continued drawing from in subsequent months. An external recruiter had previously quoted 2 weeks and EGP 2,000 per hire — and produced zero results.
